Program Overview

Eight weeks. One cohesive path to transformation.

This holistic initiative removes financial barriers and delivers wraparound support in fitness, nutrition, mental health, and community accountability.

Before you start

Medical clearance

Complete the Physical Activity Readiness Questionnaire (PAR-Q) and provide medical documentation if required, so you’re cleared for the program.

Saturday, April 4, 2026 · 10:30 AM

Kick-off event

Program begins at The Mali Center, 576 Front Avenue, St. Paul. Meet your cohort, the fitness and wellness team, and get oriented to the 8-week journey.

Weeks 1–8

Core program

Three in-person workouts per week at The F.I.T. Lab (90% attendance goal), plus mental health and nutrition sessions (virtual, hybrid, or in person). Build habits with your cohort and certified professionals.

Saturday, May 30, 2026

Completion & incentives

Challenge ends with final assessments. Successful completers receive full $150 fee reimbursement, a one-year membership to the St. Paul YMCA East Side, and two $500 stipends are awarded to the most engaged participants.

Fitness for all levels

Six workout options weekly led by certified professionals, including yoga and mobility.

Nutrition education that sticks

Guided by Julie McMahon with cooking demos, market tours, and practical strategies.

Mental health & emotional wellness

Culturally responsive therapists supporting sustainable change in real-world contexts.

Access without financial burden

$150 program fee reimbursed on completion; 95%+ of costs covered by partners.

Tyrone Minor

Tyrone is the founder and CEO of The Mali Center, a holistic wellness hub empowering health and diverse entrepreneurship in the North End and surrounding communities of St. Paul. Michelle Dalluge

ACE Certified Personal Trainer, Owner of Aspire Fitness

Dedicated to helping others be physically and mentally healthy; part of The F.I.T. Lab community for over 10 years.

Michelle Dalluge is an ACE certified personal trainer who has dedicated her life to helping others be physically and mentally healthy. She has a bachelor’s in physical and health education, a master’s in Developmental Kinesiology, and is the owner of Aspire Fitness.

Michelle does not just train her clients on how to be healthier and stronger—she also educates them that age is just a number. Being a 50+ mother of three who gained over 70 lbs with each pregnancy, she knows the struggles, especially for women who face taking care of their families along with everyday life. Her philosophy is that getting older does not mean it is too late to start your fitness journey. She educates her clients on how important strength training is as we get older and enjoys training or teaching classes to help them become stronger and feel empowered. She will push you beyond where you thought you would go and be right there with you to celebrate your successes. Her motto is “stronger than yesterday.” The first step is to walk through the door to start your fitness journey; once you have started, there is no looking back—no matter your age, gender, or fitness level, there is nothing that you can’t do.

Aspire Fitness is not just about being healthy and stronger physically; it is also about making the community stronger. Michelle has volunteered in several community events to immerse herself in the great community that surrounds The Mali Center. Aspire Fitness hosted a family fun night where youth in the community were supplied with backpacks and school supplies to start the school year. Helping others, whether becoming physically stronger or making our community stronger, is what makes Michelle passionate in what she does.

Michelle has been a part of The F.I.T. Lab community for over 10 years. She not only trains clients to become stronger through strength training but also practices what she teaches. Strength training and living a healthy lifestyle are part of who she is. Aspire Fitness currently offers personal training and a women’s cohort small group class for women who want to start or continue a strength training program, become healthier, and work with other women with similar goals.

Eligibility

East Side residency required for reimbursement & stipends.

Participants must show proof of East Side residency to qualify for the fee reimbursement and winner’s stipend.

Accepted proof of residency

  • Government-issued ID (e.g. MN Driver’s License)
  • Utility bills (water, gas, electric)
  • Signed residential lease agreement
  • Mortgage document or landlord statement
  • Notarized affidavit of residency
  • Official mail (e.g. tax forms, benefits history)

Requirements: A physical address is required; P.O. boxes are not accepted. Documents must be current (e.g. utility bills within 90 days).
